WELCOME
The Mayor acknowledged we are on the unceded homelands of the Musqueam, Squamish, and
Tsleil-Waututh People. We thank them for having cared for this land and look forward to working
with them in partnership as we continue to build this great city together.
The Mayor also recognized the immense contributions of the City of Vancouver’s team
members who work hard every day to help make our city an incredible place to live, work, and
play.
PROCLAMATION – RAMADAN

2. Report Back: Gastown Water Street Pedestrian Zone Pilot Update
January 28, 2025
Staff from Engineering Services provided a presentation and responded to questions.
Council heard from fourteen speakers, six who spoke in support of the report recommendations,
one who spoke in opposition, and seven who spoke to other aspects of the report.
MOVED by Councillor Kirby-Yung
SECONDED by Councillor Klassen
THAT Council direct staff to implement a 2025 Water Street Pedestrian Zone pilot from
early June to early September after the September long-weekend as detailed in the
Report dated January 28, 2025, entitled “Report Back: Gastown Water Street Pedestrian
Zone Pilot Update”, including:
a. Improvements to the Water Street Experience;
b. Implementation of two-way traffic on Cordova Street from Richards Street
to Gore Avenue; and,
c. Delivery of Pedestrian Zone on Water Street from Columbia Street to
Richards Street, on Sundays notionally from 10 am to 6 pm subject to
alignment with the implementation of two-way traffic on Cordova Street in
“b” above;
FURTHER THAT staff continue to advance the Gastown Public Spaces Plan and near-
term investments, including Trounce Alley rehabilitation, brick sidewalk repairs, and the
installation of a public washroom;

5. Housing and Homelessness Services 2025 Grants: Renter Services Grants and
Update to SRO Upgrading Grant Recipient
January 28, 2025
MOVED by Councillor Kirby-Yung
SECONDED by Councillor Dominato
A. THAT Council approve 14 Renter Services Grants totalling $500,000 from the
2025 Housing and Homelessness Services Operating Budget (“EHT Allocation”),
to the organizations listed in Appendix A of the Report dated January 28, 2025,
entitled “Housing and Homelessness Services 2025 Grants: Renter Services
Grants and Update to SRO Upgrading Grant Recipient”, for the amounts
recommended for each organization in the "2025 Recommend" column.
B. THAT Council allocate $500,000 from the Empty Homes Tax Reserve to
continue the Renter Services Grants program for 2026 to enable non-profit
organizations to provide critical programs and services to Vancouver renters at
risk of displacement from their homes.
C. THAT, pursuant to Section 206(1)(j) of the Vancouver Charter, Council deems
any organization that is to receive a grant pursuant to the above
recommendations, which is not a registered charity with Canada Revenue
Agency, to be an organization contributing to the health and welfare of the City.
D. THAT Council delegate its authority to execute grant agreements that include
terms and conditions that are consistent with the Report dated January 28, 2025,
entitled “Housing and Homelessness Services 2025 Grants: Renter Services
Grants and Update to SRO Upgrading Grant Recipient”, that satisfy the City’s
Director of Legal Services, and that disburse the grants described in this report,
to the City’s General Manager of Arts, Culture, and Community Services (or their
designate).
E. THAT pursuant to Section 206(1)(j) of the Vancouver Charter, Council deem the
Natives of Toi Shan Benevolent Society (“Toi Shan”) to be an organization
contributing to the health and welfare of Vancouver.
F. THAT no legal rights or obligations will arise or be created by Council’s adoption
of A through C above unless and until all legal documentation has been executed
and delivered by the respective parties.
carried
Prior to the vote, Council agreed to separate the vote on the components of the motion. A and C
were put and CARRIED AND A BY THE REQUIRED MAJORITY (Vote No. 10546), with
Councillor Montague opposed, and B, D, E and F were then put and CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY
AND B AND E BY THE REQUIRED MAJORITY (Vote No. 10547).
