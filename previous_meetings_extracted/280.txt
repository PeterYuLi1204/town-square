WELCOME
The Chair acknowledged we are on the unceded homelands of the Musqueam, Squamish, and
Tsleil-Waututh People. We thank them for having cared for this land and look forward to
working with them in partnership as we continue to build this great city together.

1. 2023 Report #6 – Vancouver Police Department’s Enterprise Risk Management
The Auditor General and the Deputy Auditor General provided a presentation of the item and,
along with the Chair of the Governance Committee, Vancouver Police Board and
Superintendent Sideroff of the Vancouver Police Department, responded to questions.
During questions to staff, it was,
MOVED by Councillor Dominato
SECONDED by Councillor Kirby-Yung
THAT under Section 5.4(d) of the Procedure By-law, the Committee approve the asking
of a second round of questions on item 1.
CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY
Following questions to staff, it was,
MOVED by Councillor Dominato
SECONDED by Councillor Kirby-Yung
THAT the Committee recommend to Council
THAT the Auditor General Committee receive the Auditor General’s report dated
December 2023, entitled “Vancouver Police Department’s Enterprise Risk
Management"; and
FURTHER THAT the above-noted report’s nine recommendations be endorsed.
CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY (Vote No. 09754)
2. Whistleblower Update
The Auditor General provided a verbal update of the item, as well as noted plans for the Auditor
General’s office, including staff recruitment, projects, and processes. Further, the Auditor
General responded to questions.
