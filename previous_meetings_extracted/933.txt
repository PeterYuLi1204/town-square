WELCOME
The Chair acknowledged we are on the unceded territories of the Musqueam, Squamish, and
Tsleil-Waututh Nations and we thank them for having cared for this land and look forward to
working with them in partnership as we continue to build this great city together.
The Chair also recognized the immense contributions of the City of Vancouver’s staff who work
hard every day to help make our city an incredible place to live, work, and play.
MATTERS ADOPTED ON CONSENT
At 9:41 am, Councillor Dominato declared conflict of interest with regard to the grant for the
Kettle Friendship Society contained in Item 3, as having previously served on the Society’s
Board.
Subsequently, the Committee considered Item 3 as the first order of business on the agenda
and agreed to separate the components of the motion for the vote.

1. Interim Report – Women’s Equity and Trans, Gender Variant and Two-Spirit
Inclusion
April 9, 2019
Staff from Arts, Culture and Community Services, Human Resources and Engineering Services
provided a presentation, and along with the City Manager and Ellen Woodsworth from Women
Transforming Cities, responded to questions.

Standing Committee of Council on City Finance and Services
Minutes, Wednesday, May 29, 2019 4
MOVED by Councillor Carr
THAT the Committee recommend to Council
A. THAT Council receive the Administrative Report dated April 9, 2019, entitled
“Interim Report – Women’s Equity and Trans, Gender Variant and Two-Spirit
Inclusion”, for information.
B. THAT Council direct staff to send the Administrative Report dated April 9, 2019,
entitled “Interim Report – Women’s Equity and Trans, Gender Variant and Two-
Spirit Inclusion”, to the Vancouver Board of Parks and Recreation, Vancouver
Public Library Board and Vancouver Police Department Board for information.
amended
AMENDMENT MOVED by Councillor Swanson
THAT the following be added as C:
C. THAT Council direct staff to develop a comprehensive gendered
intersectional strategy with short and long-term goals that are measurable
for each department and every strategy, and supported for at least six (6)
years.
CARRIED (Vote No. 04501)
(Councillor Wiebe opposed)
(Councillor Hardwick absent for the vote)
AMENDMENT MOVED by Councillor Dominato
THAT B be amended by inserting the words “, Vancouver Board of Education” after the
words “Library Board”; and
FURTHER THAT the following be added as D:
D. THAT further to the motion dated November 28, 2017, entitled “Equitable
Parental Leave at the City of Vancouver”, Council direct staff to arrange a
briefing on this matter to explore options for achieving this goal.
CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY (Vote No. 04502)
(Councillors Bligh, Boyle and Hardwick absent for the vote)
The amendments having carried, the motion as amended was put and CARRIED
UNANIMOUSLY with Councillor Hardwick absent for the vote (Vote No. 04503).
FINAL MOTION AS APPROVED
A. THAT Council receive the Administrative Report dated April 9, 2019, entitled
“Interim Report – Women’s Equity and Trans, Gender Variant and Two-Spirit
Inclusion”, for information.

Standing Committee of Council on City Finance and Services
Minutes, Wednesday, May 29, 2019 5
B. THAT Council direct staff to send the Administrative Report dated April 9, 2019,
entitled “Interim Report – Women’s Equity and Trans, Gender Variant and Two-

2. Theatre Rental Grant Allocations – Fall 2019 and Spring 2020
April 30, 2019
The Committee heard from one speaker in general support of the recommendations, and one
speaker who offered general comments.
MOVED by Councillor Bligh
THAT the Committee recommend to Council
A. THAT Council approve 40 Theatre Rental Grants to the institutions listed in the
Organization column of Appendix A in the Administrative Report dated
April 30, 2019, entitled “Theatre Rental Grant Allocations – Fall 2019 and Spring
2020”, in the amounts set out beside their names in the “Sept 1-Dec 31, 2019
TRG Recommendation” column of the table in Appendix A for use of the Civic
Theatres from September 1-December 31, 2019, in an aggregate amount of
$591,620; source of funding is the remaining balance in the 2019 Theatre Rental
Grant Budget.
B. THAT Council approve, in advance of the 2020 operating budget, 53 Theatre
Rental Grants to the institutions listed in the Organization column of Appendix B
in the Administrative Report dated April 30, 2019, entitled “Theatre Rental Grant
Allocations – Fall 2019 and Spring 2020”, in the amounts set out beside their
names in the “Jan 1– Aug 31, 2020 TRG Recommendation” column of the table
in Appendix B for use of the Civic Theatres from January 1-August 31, 2020, in
an aggregate amount of $967,590; source of funding is the 2020 Theatre Rental
Grants Budget.
C. THAT, pursuant to Section 206 (1) (j) of the Vancouver Charter, Council deems
any organization listed in Appendix A or Appendix B of the Administrative Report
dated April 30, 2019, entitled “Theatre Rental Grant Allocations – Fall 2019 and
Spring 2020”, that is not otherwise a charity registered with the Canada Revenue
Agency to be contributing to the culture of Vancouver.

Standing Committee of Council on City Finance and Services
Minutes, Wednesday, May 29, 2019 6
D. THAT the General Manager of Arts, Culture and Community Services (GM of
ACCS) is authorized to negotiate and execute agreements to disburse the grants
described in the Administrative Report dated April 30, 2019, entitled “Theatre
Rental Grant Allocations – Fall 2019 and Spring 2020”, on the terms and
conditions generally set out below, and on such other terms and conditions as
are satisfactory to the GM of ACCS and the City Solicitor.
E. THAT no legal rights or obligations are created by the approval of A and B above
unless and until the applicable theatre license agreement is complete and
approved by the City in accordance with D above and executed and delivered by
both the grant recipient and GM of ACCS (or their designate).
CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY AND
BY THE REQUIRED MAJORITY (Vote No. 04504)

4. Contract Award for Consultant for Arbutus Greenway Character Zones Three and
Eight
April 29, 2019
Staff from Engineering Services responded to questions.
MOVED by Councillor Wiebe
THAT the Committee recommend to Council
A. THAT Council authorize City staff to negotiate to the satisfaction of the General
Manager of Engineering Services, the Director of Legal Services, and the Chief
Procurement Office, to enter into a contract with PWL Partnership Landscape
Architects Inc., for the supply of consultant services for detailed design of the
Arbutus Greenway Character Zones Three and Eight. The contract will be for a
term of three (3) years. The award recommendation is for $2,566,091.40, plus
applicable taxes.
B. THAT the General Manager of Engineering Services, the Director of Legal
Services and the Chief Procurement Officer, be authorized to execute on behalf
of the City the contract as outlined in A above.
C. THAT no legal rights or obligations will be created by Council’s adoption of A and
B above unless and until such contract is executed by the authorized signatories
of the City as set out in these recommendations.
CARRIED (Vote No. 04505)
(Councillor Hardwick opposed)
The Committee adjourned at 12:03 pm.
