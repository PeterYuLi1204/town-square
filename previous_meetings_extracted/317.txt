WELCOME
The Mayor acknowledged we are on the unceded homelands of the Musqueam, Squamish, and
Tsleil-Waututh People. We thank them for having cared for this land and look forward to working
with them in partnership as we continue to build this great city together.
The Mayor also recognized the immense contributions of the City of Vancouver’s staff who work
hard every day to help make our city an incredible place to live, work, and play.

September 22, 2023
Staff from Arts, Culture and Community Services responded to questions.
MOVED by Councillor Meiszner
SECONDED by Councillor Montague
A. THAT Council designate the Accessibility Committee as a Type C Civic Agency,
in accordance with the Accessible British Columbia Act.
B. THAT Council approve the proposed compensation for Accessibility Committee
members in accordance with the revised Terms of Reference found in Appendix
A, similar to the remuneration structure for the Board of Variance.
amended
AMENDMENT MOVED by Councillor Kirby-Yung
SECONDED by Councillor Montague
THAT the following be added as C:

Council Meeting
Minutes, October 3, 2023 14
C. THAT Council acknowledges the new Provincially mandated requirement for
an Accessibility Committee while also noting that it overlaps with the scope of the
City’s existing Persons with Disabilities Advisory Committee;
FURTHER THAT Council direct staff to engage with the Persons with Disabilities
Advisory Committee in light of the overlapping committee scopes to gauge their
interest in sitting on the new Provincially mandated Accessibility Committee in
the future, with additional direction to staff to report back with recommendations
before the next open call for Advisory Committee members.
amended
AMENDMENT TO THE AMENDMENT MOVED by Councillor Carr
SECONDED by Councillor Boyle
THAT C be amended be inserted the words “and Older Persons and Elders Advisory
Committee” after the words “Persons with Disabilities Advisory Committee”.
CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY (Vote No. 9505)
(Councillors Bligh and Dominato absent for the vote)
The amendment to the amendment have carried, the amended amendment was put and
CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY (Vote No. 9506), with Councillors Bligh and Dominato absent for the
vote. Following discussion, the motion as amended was put and CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY
(Vote No. 9507), with Councillors Bligh and Dominato absent for the vote.
FINAL MOTION AS APPROVED
A. THAT Council designate the Accessibility Committee as a Type C Civic Agency,
in accordance with the Accessible British Columbia Act.
B. THAT Council approve the proposed compensation for Accessibility Committee
members in accordance with the revised Terms of Reference found in
Appendix A of the Report dated September 22, 2023, entitled “Accessibility
Committee: Designation and Remuneration”, similar to the remuneration
structure for the Board of Variance.
C. THAT Council acknowledges the new Provincially mandated requirement for
an Accessibility Committee while also noting that it overlaps with the scope of the
City’s existing Persons with Disabilities Advisory Committee and Older Persons
and Elders Advisory Committee;
FURTHER THAT Council direct staff to engage with the Persons with Disabilities
Advisory Committee and Older Persons and Elders Advisory Committee in light
of the overlapping committee scopes to gauge their interest in sitting on the new
Provincially mandated Accessibility Committee in the future, with additional
direction to staff to report back with recommendations before the next open call
for Advisory Committee members.

in the current Capital Plan for the project, however, funding will be included in future Capital
Plans. He agreed to follow-up with an update.
