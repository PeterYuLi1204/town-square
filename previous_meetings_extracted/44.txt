SPECIAL COUNCIL MEETING MINUTES
(BUSINESS LICENCE HEARING)
OCTOBER 14, 2025
A Special Meeting of the Council of the City of Vancouver was held on Tuesday, October 14,
2025, at 9:36 am, in the Council Chamber, Third Floor, City Hall, for the purpose of holding a
Business Licence Hearing to consider the decision of the Chief Licence Inspector to not support
the issuance of a 2025 business licence (“Business Licence”) to Ping Wu (the “Applicant”) for a
short-term rental accommodation at 701-989 Nelson Street (the “Premises”). This Special
Council meeting was convened in person and via electronic means as authorized by Part 14 of
the Procedure By-law and the Vancouver Charter.
PRESENT: Councillor Lucy Maloney, Chair

Robert LeBlanc, Litigation Counsel, Legal Services, was present on behalf of the City of
Vancouver. Ping Wu, the Applicant, was present on her own behalf. Ping Wu was assisted by
Mandarin interpreter, Dorothy Jia.
Mr. LeBlanc provided opening remarks and reviewed relevant legislation as set out in section
275 of the Vancouver Charter and section(s) 3(17) of the City of Vancouver’s Licence By-law
No. 4450.
In support of the allegations set out in the evidence brief, Mr. LeBlanc called Arlina Urloiu,
Deputy Chief Licence Inspector, as a witness for the City of Vancouver.
Mr. LeBlanc along with Panel members directed questions to the witness.
The Applicant provided opening remarks and responded to the allegations submitted by the
witness.
The Applicant, as the witness, responded to questions from the Panel.
Mr. LeBlanc provided closing submissions based on the evidence and reviewed the reasons for
the business licence denial.
The Applicant provided closing submissions.
PANEL MEMBERS DISCUSSION
MOVED by Councillor Bligh
SECONDED by Councillor Montague
THAT the Business Licence Hearing Panel overturn the decision of the Chief Licence
Inspector to not support the issuance of a 2025 business licence (“Business Licence”) to
Ping Wu (the “Applicant”) for a short-term rental accommodation at 701-989 Nelson
Street and that the Business Licence be amended to include the following conditions:
• THAT the City of Vancouver continue the verification process to ensure all
short-term rental operations and subsequent application at 701-989 Nelson
Street are in order and fully compliant with the requirements set out in the
