2. Single-Use Item Reduction Strategy By-laws – Consultation Update
April 16, 2019
On April 24, 2019, Council referred this matter to the reconvening Standing Committee meeting on April
29, 2019, with a hard start of 3:00 pm.
On April 29, 2019, the Committee heard from 7 speakers in general support of the recommendations
and 3 speakers in general opposition to the recommendations.
MOVED by Councillor Fry
THAT the Committee recommend to Council
A. THAT the Director of Legal Services be instructed to bring forward for enactment
amendments to the License By-law (No. 4450) and Ticket Offences By-law (No. 9360),
generally in accordance with Appendix C, prohibiting the distribution of prepared food in
polystyrene foam cups and polystyrene foam take-out containers by business license
holders beginning January 1, 2020, including temporary exemptions for charitable food
providers.
B. THAT Council postpone the effective date from June 1, 2019, for proposed by-law
amendments prohibiting the distribution of plastic straws until April 2020 and direct staff
to report back by November 30, 2019, with details informed by stakeholder consultation
for single-use item by-law requirements, including provisions for ensuring accessibility.
C. THAT Council direct staff to report back by November 30, 2019:
i. A summary of stakeholder feedback received through current consultation
on proposed by-law amendments for single-use items including plastic
and paper shopping bags, disposable cups, take-out containers, plastic
straws and single-use utensils;
ii. The results of staff’s analysis of stakeholder feedback and relevant
technical and legal considerations;

Standing Committee of Council on City Finance and Services
Minutes, April 24, and 29, 2019 4
iii. Recommendations regarding by-law amendments including
implementation and enforcement plans, and program delivery including
resources and additional stakeholder engagement that may be required;
and
iv. New options for a plastic bag ban that consider both how legal and public
opinion has changed since Victoria’s plastic bag ban, and the growing
interest among neighbouring municipalities to collaborate on a plastic bag
ban. This expedited report back should consider the Victoria approach to
plastic bag ban, stakeholder consultations to date, and the merits and
options around a plastic bag ban, versus fees or a modified custom plan.
D. THAT Council direct staff to bring forward two (2) resolutions for the 2019 Union of
British Columbia Municipalities Convention requesting the provincial government’s
support in addressing single-use items by:
i. Adopting a more comprehensive provincial single-use item reduction
strategy, as provided in Appendix E; and
ii. Developing provincial standards for compostable single-use items
ensuring that they are designed to fully biodegrade if littered in the natural
environment, that any standards and certifications for compostability are
aligned with provincial composting infrastructure, and that compostable
single-use items are collected and managed through an extended
producer responsibility program that covers the residential and
commercial sectors as well as materials from the public realm, as
provided in Appendix F.
E. THAT the Mayor write to the Chair of the Greater Vancouver Sewer and Drainage District
Board and the Ministry of Environment and Climate Change Strategy requesting support
for the development of a Provincial Single-Use Item Reduction Strategy aligned with
Federal goals for reduction of plastic waste.
amended
AMENDMENT MOVED by Councillor Wiebe
THAT B be deleted and replace with the following:
THAT Council postpone the effective date from June 1, 2019, for proposed by-law
amendments prohibiting the non-necessary use of non-compostable utensils and plastic
straws on January 1, 2020 and direct staff to report back by November 30, 2019, with
details informed by stakeholder consultation for single-use item by-law requirements,
including provisions for ensuring accessibility, including the City looking at options in
supporting businesses for a small amount of accessible friendly straws. That the report
back with communications that includes viable alternatives and their associated impact

April 12, 2019
On April 24, 2019, staff from Planning, Urban Design and Sustainability, responded to questions.

renew for the second time the Heritage Energy Retrofit Grant program for owners of

Standing Committee of Council on City Finance and Services

B. THAT Council affirm and acknowledge there is a financial impact and many small
independent business are facing difficulty with the current structure of how non-
residential property classes (including classes 2, 4, 5 and 6) are taxed.
C. THAT Council acknowledges the unaffordability residential property owners,
including co-ops and non-profit housing providers face in the City of Vancouver
and the implications of shifting more tax and cost onto property owners;
FURTHER THAT Council acknowledge the impact this may have on housing
affordability including any impact on renters, considering rent for current tenants
cannot be raised above the rate of inflation.
D. THAT Council direct the intergovernmental working group to consider other
financial models that may provide relief to small independent and locally owned
small businesses, including reviewing the possibility of amending class 6
property tax to shift tax revenue from large multinational and internationally
owned businesses to small independent and locally owned businesses.
E. THAT Council direct the intergovernmental working group to come back with
recommendations by June 2019 to create a Small Business Task Force,
including stakeholders with the mandate to produce a report to send to the
Minister of Finance for the Province of British Columbia, requesting the Province
to consider ways for BC Assessment to work with the City of Vancouver to
explore possible ways to achieve tax savings for small, independent and locally
owned small businesses, including legacy businesses and recognizing their
value in the City of Vancouver.
