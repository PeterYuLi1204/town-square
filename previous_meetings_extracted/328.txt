WELCOME
The Chair acknowledged we are on the unceded homelands of the Musqueam, Squamish, and
Tsleil-Waututh People. We thank them for having cared for this land and look forward to working
with them in partnership as we continue to build this great city together.
The Chair also recognized the immense contributions of the City of Vancouver’s staff who work
hard every day to help make our city an incredible place to live, work, and play.

FINAL MOTION AS APPROVED
A. THAT Council approve initial multi-year capital budget and funding adjustments
for six projects as outlined in the Report dated July 11, 2023, entitled “Provincial
‘Growing Communities Fund’: Initial Allocation to City Projects”, which will result
in:
a) An increase of $22.5 million to the 2023-2026 Capital Plan; and
b) An increase of $25 million to the Multi-Year Capital Project
Budgets.
Funding for these budget adjustments will come from the grant received from the
Provincial Government’s Growing Communities Fund. Details of the proposed
adjustments can be found in Appendix A of the above-noted report.
B. THAT staff report back with recommendations for the allocation of the remaining
$23.6 million of the Growing Communities Fund no later than the 2023-2026
Capital Plan Mid-term Update scheduled for mid-2024, prioritizing access to
washrooms in the public realm and accessibility and inclusion improvements as
found in Appendix B of the Report dated July 11, 2023, entitled “Provincial
‘Growing Communities Fund’: Initial Allocation to City Projects”..
C. THAT Council direct staff to include Parks within the priorities for the initial
Growing Communities fund grant, and that a portion of funding for temporary
plazas be used to fund and support the completion of a new turf field at Moberly
Park and a spray park at Ross Park, both of which have been identified as a
priority by the South Vancouver community;
FURTHER THAT staff conduct the appropriate analysis and report back to
Council regarding the scope, cost, and delivery timelines of these projects.

ii. Working with CMHC-Granville Island and senior governments to seek

2. 2023 Annual Inflationary Rate Adjustment to Development Cost Levies
July 11, 2023
THAT the Committee recommend to Council
THAT Council approve, in-principle, a 2023 inflationary rate adjustment of 8.3% for the
Vancouver Development Cost Levy By-law, the Vancouver Utilities Development Cost
Levy By-law and the Area Specific Development Cost Levy By-law;
FURTHER THAT, in recognition that in 2022 Council approved a two-phase
Development Cost Levy increase with the second phase scheduled to come into effect
on September 30, 2023, the implementation of the 2023 inflationary rate increase be
deferred and included as part of the 2024 annual inflationary rate adjustment.
ADOPTED ON CONSENT (Vote No. 09447)
(Councillor Dominato absent for the vote)

5. Contract Award for Green Bin Organics Processing
July 12, 2023
Staff from Finance, Risk and Supply Chain Management and Engineering Services responded
to questions.
Council heard from four speakers who spoke in opposition of the recommendations.
MOVED by Councillor Klassen
SECONDED by Councillor Dominato
THAT the Committee recommend to Council
A. THAT, having been advised of the Notice of Intent to Contract that the City issued
on June 16, 2023 (the “NOITC”) to inform the market about the City’s intent to
enter into a contract with GFL Environmental Inc. (“GFL”) for the receipt and
processing of organics for a term of five years and with an estimated value of
$30,800,000 plus applicable taxes (“Contract”) and the complaints that the City
received from vendors in response to the NOITC, Council authorizes staff to
proceed with the permitted sole source process for the Contract pursuant to the
City’s Procurement Policy (ADMIN-008).
B. THAT the City’s Director of Legal Services, Chief Procurement Officer and
General Manager of Engineering Services be authorized to negotiate the
Contract to their satisfaction and in accordance with the Report dated July 12,
2023, entitled “Contract Award for Green Bin Organics Processing”, and be
delegated authority to execute the Contract on behalf of the City.
C. THAT Council directs City staff to:
i. engage with industry through a market sounding or similar procurement
process for the purpose of informing the City’s long term planning for
organics processing; and

Standing Committee of Council on Policy and Strategic Priorities
Minutes, Wednesday, July 26, 2023 8
ii. report back to Council in 2025 with the results of the market engagement
process described above and recommendations for the City’s future
organics processing strategy.
D. THAT no legal rights or obligations will be created by Council’s adoption of A and
B above unless and until the City and GFL Environmental Inc. execute the
Contract.
