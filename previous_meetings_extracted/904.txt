1. Culture|Shift: Blanketing the City in Arts and Culture: Vancouver Culture Plan
2020-2029
August 13, 2018
Sandra Singh, General Manager, Arts, Culture and Community Services, introduced the report
Culture|Shift, a 10 year culture plan framework with strategic directions and actions to align and
increase support for art and culture. Ms. Singh invited Christie Lee Charles, Poet Laureate and
Musqueam Nation Liaison to the Culture Plan to open the presentation with a welcome.
Culture|Shift incorporates several related plans in support for art and culture. The following staff
from Arts, Culture and Community Services presented on the following:
• Culture|Shift - Metha Brown, Social Planner, Cultural Services;
• Making Space for Arts and Culture - Alix Sales, Senior Cultural Planner, Cultural
Spaces and Infrastructure, Cultural Services; and
• Vancouver Music Strategy - Jarrett Martineau, Social Planner, Cultural Services.
Branislav Henselmann, Managing Director, Cultural Services, provided an overview of financial
implications and outcomes of the Culture|Shift report.
Council heard from 16 speakers in support of the recommendations.

2. Special Events Policy Framework
August 30, 2019
Margaret Wittgens, Director, Public Space and Street Use, Engineering Services, along with
Geoff Teoli, Branch Manager, Film and Special Events, provided an overview of the updated
Special Events Policy Framework that includes improvements to process and operations,
financial investments and capacity building.
Council heard from nine speakers in support of the recommendations and one spoke on other
matters.
Ms. Wittgens, along with Mr. Teoli and Octavio Silva, Manager, Business Development, Parks
and Recreation, responded to questions.
MOVED by Councillor De Genova
SECONDED by Councillor Carr
A. THAT Council approve the new special events policy framework (the “New SEP”)
as generally described in the Policy Report dated August 30, 2019, entitled
“Special Event Policy Framework”, and that the City Manager be authorized to
settle and approve the detailed policy document required to implement the New
SEP on such terms and conditions as are outlined in the above-noted report and
on such other terms and conditions as are considered satisfactory to the City
Manager, including the sub-delegation of authority to other officials and staff to
approve or execute application forms, guidelines, and legal agreements.
B. THAT, subject to approval of A above, Council direct staff to bring forward a
proposal for funding of $730,000 for consideration in the 2020 budget process to
support the New SEP.
C. THAT, subject to the approval of A and B above, Council approve in principle the
allocation of the $730,000 as follows:

Regular Council Meeting
Minutes, September 10, 2019 6
a. $300,000 for special event public safety, subject to review annually so that
public safety measures outlined in the Policy Report dated August 30, 2019,
entitled “Special Event Policy Framework”, are not invoiced to event
organizers; and
b. $430,000 to increase the City operational funding for special events (as
defined in the Policy Report dated August 30, 2019, entitled “Special Event
Policy Framework”), currently $2,000 for qualifying events, to be 75% of City
costs up to a maximum of $75,000 for qualifying events, as outlined in this
report.
D. THAT, subject to the approval of A to C above,
a. the City Manager complete the documentation and implementation of the
New SEP so as to be reported back to Council by the end Q4 2019 and take
effect for special events starting January 1, 2020.
b. the New SEP be structured so as to ensure that the City’s Operational and
Public Safety Costs with respect to special events does not exceed the
annual budget amount approved by Council subject to annual review.
E. THAT Council direct staff to review the green events program to develop
requirements for the provision of green power plans for special events beginning
January 2020, in order to work toward eliminating reliance on generators, and to
include these in the policy document to be created in A above.
F. THAT Council direct staff to explore ways to offset incremental costs associated
with the Special Events Policy Framework;
FURTHER THAT Council direct staff to do this in a way that does not compete
with sponsorship efforts of local event organizers and report back to Council in
2020 with an update.
