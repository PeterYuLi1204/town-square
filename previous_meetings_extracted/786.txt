WELCOME
The Chair acknowledged we are on the unceded territories of the Musqueam, Squamish, and
Tsleil-Waututh Nations and we thank them for having cared for this land and look forward to
working with them in partnership as we continue to build this great city together.
The Chair also recognized the immense contributions of the City of Vancouver’s staff who work
hard every day to help make our city an incredible place to live, work, and play.

1. Contract Award for Supply and Maintenance of Sewage Heat Recovery Heat Pump
July 8, 2020
THAT the Committee recommend to Council
A. THAT Council authorize City staff to negotiate to the satisfaction of the City’s
General Manager of Engineering, City’s Director of Legal Services, and the City’s
Chief Procurement Officer and enter into a contract with Johnson Controls
Canada LP, for the supply and maintenance of a fully packaged sewage heat
recovery heat pump that will achieve 6.9 megawatt of thermal output for a term of
10 years with an estimated contract value of $6,581,726.50, plus applicable
taxes over the term; $5,161,800.50 to be funded through approved multi-year
NEU Waste Heat Recovery Expansion Capital Project Budget and the balance,
$1,419,926, from ongoing annual operating budgets via the NEU Rates over the
10 year term.
B. THAT the Director of Legal Services, Chief Procurement Officer and General
Manager of Engineering be authorized to execute on behalf of the City the
contract contemplated by A above.
C. THAT no legal rights or obligations will be created by Council’s adoption of A and
B above unless and until such contract is executed by the authorized signatories
of the City as set out.
ADOPTED ON CONSENT (Vote No. 06188)

MOVED by Councillor Hardwick
THAT the Committee recommend to Council
A. THAT Council receive for information the 2021 Budget Outlook.
B. THAT Council direct staff to develop a comprehensive “environmental risk
assessment” related to the coronavirus and the range of impacts it could have on
the City’s operations and finances in 2021 and beyond, including:
• potential impacts on revenues and expenditures in the Operating Budget;
• an assessment of health of the local economy and its ability to support
existing and new civic programs;

Standing Committee of Council on City Finance and Services
Minutes, Wednesday, July 8, 2020 4
• identification of any new short or long term priorities that should inform
development of 2021 service plans and budgets and where they should fit
in Council’s overall direction for developing the 2021 Budget;
FURTHER THAT that the report be brought for Council consideration before
October 1, 2020.
C. THAT Council direct staff to develop the first iteration of the 2021 Budget based
on the decisions arising from the assessment requested in Recommendation B
and options from cost of living increase (COLI) to a maximum 5% property tax
increase including different scenario options for amended service levels already
approved or newly requested for 2021.
D. THAT Council direct staff to provide the report set out in Recommendation C to
Council for information in late November and to schedule consideration and
debate during January 2021, with final approval of the budget to be scheduled
before February 28, 2021.
E. THAT Council confirm support for the recommendations of the Federation of
Canadian Municipalities for senior government support to major cities impacted
by COVID-19 to address significant funding shortfalls and enable a successful
restart and recovery program.
amended
AMENDMENT MOVED by Councillor Boyle
THAT the motion be struck and replaced with the following:
A. THAT Council receive for information the 2021-2025 Budget Outlook.
B. THAT Council endorse the priorities proposed in the 2021-2025 Budget
Outlook for staff to reference in developing the 2021-2025 Service Plans,
and the 2021 Budget and Five-Year Financial Plan.
C. THAT Council direct staff to bring forward a proposed 2021 Budget and
Five-Year Financial Plan within a tax increase of 5% that supports
Council’s priorities. For the median single-family home assessed at
$1.6M, this would result in a $146 increase in tax in 2021.
D. THAT Council confirm support for the recommendations of the Federation
of Canadian Municipalities for senior government support to major cities
impacted by COVID-19 to address significant funding shortfalls and
enable a successful restart and recovery program.
CARRIED (Vote No. 06189)
(Councillors Dominato and Hardwick opposed)
(Councillor Bligh abstaining from the vote)

FINAL MOTION AS APPROVED
A. THAT Council receive for information the 2021-2025 Budget Outlook.
B. THAT Council endorse the priorities proposed in the 2021-2025 Budget Outlook
for staff to reference in developing the 2021-2025 Service Plans, and the 2021
Budget and Five-Year Financial Plan.
C. THAT Council direct staff to bring forward a proposed 2021 Budget and Five-
Year Financial Plan with options for a tax increase of up to 5% that supports
Council’s priorities. For the median single-family home assessed at $1.6M, a 5%
tax increase would result in a $146 increase in tax in 2021;
FURTHER THAT Council direct staff to provide the first iteration of the 2021
Budget for information in late October.
D. THAT Council confirm support for the recommendations of the Federation of
Canadian Municipalities for senior government support to major cities impacted
by COVID-19 to address significant funding shortfalls and enable a successful
restart and recovery program.
E. THAT Council direct staff to arrange and schedule separate quarterly briefings in
Q3 and Q4 from the Vancouver Park Board, Vancouver Police Department and
the Vancouver Police Board, the Vancouver Public Library and the Vancouver
Public Library Board, Vancouver Fire and Rescue Services and other key bodies
and civic unions to help inform budget decisions of Council for 2021.

MOVED by Councillor De Genova

THAT the application to amend the False Creek Area Development Plan for Area 10B
(By-law No. 5478) in order to create a new Development Area for the site located at
1595 West 2nd Avenue (1700 Fir Street) [Parcel A, False Creek Plan, BCP19152;
Parcel 1, Block 220 District Lot 526, Group 1, New Westminster District Plan,
LMP27720; Parcel 2, Block 220, District Lot 526, Group 1, New Westminster District
Plan LMP27720; PIDs: 026-380-641, 023-378-085, 023-378-093 respectively]; generally
as presented in Appendix A of the Referral Report dated March 17, 2020, entitled
“Amendments to the False Creek Area Development Plan for Area 10B” be approved.
carried
AMENDMENT MOVED by Councillor Hardwick
THAT the following be added at the end:
THAT staff ensure that sufficient space is set aside to enable the double-
tracked surface rail connections between the False Creek alignment and the
Arbutus Corridor and the Senakw Lands, Molson Lands, and Kitsilano
Point/Vanier Park.
THAT Title to Parcel “F” includes a notation to potential purchasers,
developers, and occupants that future public transit trains will operate close to
the property.
After a brief recess and conferring with Legal staff, the Mayor ruled the amendment out of
order as the proposed application does not have the ability to secure any other lands not
contained within the application including any private properties.
The amendment having been ruled out of order, the motion was put and CARRIED
(Vote No. 06202) with Councillors Hardwick and Kirby-Yung opposed and Councillors
De Genova and Dominato absent for the vote.
