SPECIAL COUNCIL MEETING MINUTES
(BUSINESS LICENCE HEARING)
NOVEMBER 18, 2025
A Special Meeting of the Council of the City of Vancouver was held on Tuesday,
November 18, 2025, at 9:31 am, in the Council Chamber, Third Floor, City Hall, for the purpose
of holding a Business Licence Hearing to consider the decision of the Deputy Chief Licence
Inspector to not support the issuance of a 2025 business licence (“Business Licence”) to Arya
Mahdavi-Baramchi (the “Applicant”) for a short-term rental accommodation at 605-1050 Burrard
Street (the “Premises”). This Special Council meeting was convened in person and via
electronic means as authorized by Part 14 of the Procedure By-law and the Vancouver Charter.
PRESENT: Councillor Sean Orr, Chair
Councillor Lucy Maloney

Robert LeBlanc, Litigation Counsel, Legal Services, was present on behalf of the City of
Vancouver. Arya Mahdavi-Baramchi, the Applicant, was present and accompanied by James
Vander Woude, Lawyer, Citadel Law Corporation, the Applicant’s Counsel. Arya Mahdavi-
Baramchi was assisted by Persian interpreter, Sara Molavi.
Mr. LeBlanc provided opening remarks and reviewed relevant legislation as set out in section
275 of the Vancouver Charter and section 25.1(3) of the City of Vancouver’s Licence By-law No.
4450.
In support of the allegations set out in the evidence brief, Mr. LeBlanc called Arlina Urloiu,
Deputy Chief Licence Inspector, as a witness for the City of Vancouver.
Mr. LeBlanc along with Panel members directed questions to the witness.
The Applicant’s Counsel also directed questions to the witness.
The Applicant’s Counsel provided opening remarks and responded to the allegations submitted
by the witness.
The Applicant’s Counsel called Arya Mahdavi-Baramchi, the Applicant, as a witness.
The Applicant’s Counsel directed questions to the witness.

The Business Licence Hearing Panel recessed at 11:58 am and reconvened at 12:02 pm.
* * * * *
Mr. LeBlanc provided closing submissions based on the evidence and reviewed the reasons for
the business licence denial.
The Applicant’s Counsel provided closing submissions.
PANEL MEMBERS DISCUSSION
MOVED by Councillor Meiszner
SECONDED by Councillor Maloney
THAT the Business Licence Hearing Panel uphold the decision of the Deputy Chief
Licence Inspector’s decision to not support the issuance of a 2025 business licence
(“Business Licence”) to Arya Mahdavi-Baramchi (the “Applicant”) for a short-term rental
accommodation at 605-1050 Burrard Street, on the basis that:
• The unit is not the Applicant’s principal residence unit as defined by the City
of Vancouver’s Licence Bylaw No. 4450.
CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY (Vote No. 11139)
