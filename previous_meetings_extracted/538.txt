SPECIAL COUNCIL MEETING MINUTES
(BUSINESS LICENCE HEARING)
FEBRUARY 2, 2022
A Special Meeting of the Council of the City of Vancouver was held on Wednesday,
February 2, 2022, at 6:10 pm, in the Council Chamber, Third Floor, City Hall, for the purpose of
holding a Business Licence Hearing, to consider the decision of the Chief Licence Inspector to
suspend the business licence (“Business Licence”) issued to Melanie Chin (the “Licencee”), for
a short-term rental accommodation at 301 - 33 West Pender Street, Vancouver, British
Columbia. This Special Council meeting was convened by electronic means as authorized by
Part 14 of the Procedure By-law and the Vancouver Charter.
PRESENT: Councillor Pete Fry, Chair
Councillor Adriane Carr
Councillor Sarah Kirby-Yung
CITY CLERK’S OFFICE: Tina Hildebrandt, Meeting Coordinator

Robert LeBlanc, City Prosecutor, Legal Services, was present on behalf of the City of
Vancouver. Melanie Chin, the Licencee, was present on her own behalf.
Mr. LeBlanc provided opening remarks, reviewed relevant legislation as set out in sections 275
and 277 of the Vancouver Charter, and section 25.1(3) of Licence By-law No. 4450, and
advised that the business licence was suspended as the Licencee has not demonstrated that
the premises are her principle residence.
In support of the allegations set out in the evidence brief, Mr. LeBlanc called Koji Miyaji,
Assistant Director, Community Standards, Development, Buildings and Licencing, as a witness.
Mr. LeBlanc directed questions to Mr. Miyaji.
Ms. Chin provided opening remarks and submitted the following additional evidence to verify
that her principle residence is 301 - 33 West Pender Street (on file in the City Clerk’s Office):
• 2022 Property Assessment Notice from BC Assessment;
• Mortgage paper from TD Canada Trust;
• Voting Instruction Form from Questrade; and
• Strata Minutes from September 2021.
Mr. LeBlanc provided closing submissions based on the evidence and reviewed the reasons for
the business licence suspension.
Ms Chin provided closing submissions.

Special Council (Business Licence Hearing)
Minutes, Wednesday, February 2, 2022 3
PANEL MEMBERS DISCUSSION
MOVED by Councillor Carr
SECONDED by Councillor Kirby-Yung
THAT the Business Licence Hearing Panel suspend the business licence (“Business
Licence”) issued to Melanie Chin (the “Licencee”), for a short-term rental
accommodation at 301 - 33 West Pender Street, Vancouver, British Columbia, on the
basis that there is a lack of supporting evidence verifying the Licencee’s principal
residence as #301 - 33 West Pender Street.
CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY
ADJOURNMENT
MOVED by Councillor Kirby-Yung
SECONDED by Councillor Carr
THAT the meeting be adjourned.
CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY
The Business Licence Hearing Panel adjourned at 7:31 pm.
