BUSINESS LICENCE HEARING MINUTES
SEPTEMBER 29, 2020
A Business Licence Hearing of the Council of the City of Vancouver was held on Tuesday,
September 29, 2019, at 9:30 am, in the Council Chamber, Third Floor, City Hall, to consider the
recommendations by the Chief Licence Officer to refuse the reinstatement of the Business
Licences for Short-Term Rental issued to the Licensee as set out in the Agenda and as
recorded in the Minutes of this Business Licence Hearing. This Council meeting was convened
by electronic means as authorized under Part 14 of the Procedure By-law and Vancouver
Charter and the Minister of Public Safety and Solicitor General of the Province of British
Columbia – Emergency Program Act, updated Ministerial Order No. M192.

Iain Dixon, Assistant Director – Regulatory Litigation, Legal Department, was present on behalf
of the City of Vancouver. Jan Koncewicz (the “Licencee”), was present and represented himself.
Mr. Dixon provided opening remarks, noting that the Licencee was not granted the Business
License due to his previous offences for carrying on a business without holding a City Licence.
Koji Miyaji, Assistant Director of Community Standards, was present as witness on behalf of the
City.
The Business Licence Hearing Panel asked questions to Mr. Miyaji.
Mr. Koncewicz provided opening statements and summarized his situation.
The Panel asked questions to Mr. Koncewicz.
The Panel asked clarifying questions to Mr. Miyaji on City regulations relating to Short-Term
Rental.
Mr. Dixon provided closing comments noting the Panel’s role is to uphold, overturn or amend
the decision of the Chief License Inspector in relation to status of the Business Licence of Mr.
Koncewicz.
Mr. Koncewicz provided closing comments, noting that the strata council is the cause of the
suspension of his Business Licence.

not put
The amendment, having not received a SECONDER, was not put to vote.
MOVED by Councillor Bligh
SECONDED by Councillor Swanson
THAT Council refuse to issue a Business License to Jan Koncewiz (the “Licencee”) for
the purposes of Short-Term Rental Accommodation at 2501 – 1050 Burrard Street on
the basis that:
• the Licencee contravened Licence By-law No. 4450 by operating a short term rental
at the Premises without a business license; and
• the Licencee pled guilty to one count of carrying on a business without a subsisting
City licence at the Provincial Court of British Columbia.
amended
AMENDMENT MOVED BY Councillor Bligh
SECONDED by Councillor Swanson
THAT the following be added to the end:
THAT the Licencee may not apply for a Short Term Rental Accommodation
business license before May 19, 2021 and Council direct the Chief Licence
Inspector to issue the business licence upon application if the Licencee meets all
other licence requirements.
CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY
The amendment having carried, the motion as amended was put and CARRIED
UNANIMOUSLY.
FINAL MOTION AS APPROVED
THAT Council refuse to issue a Business License to Jan Koncewiz (the “Licencee”) for
the purposes of Short-Term Rental Accommodation at 2501 – 1050 Burrard Street on
the basis that:
• the Licencee contravened Licence By-law No. 4450 by operating a short term
rental at the Premises without a business license; and
• the Licencee pled guilty to one count of carrying on a business without a
subsisting City licence at the Provincial Court of British Columbia.
