SPECIAL COUNCIL MEETING MINUTES
DECEMBER 1 AND 2, 2021
A Special Meeting of the Council of the City of Vancouver was held on Wednesday,
December 1, 2021, at 9:32 am, in the Council Chamber, Third Floor, City Hall, for the purpose
of hearing from speakers on the 2022 Capital and Operating Budget. Subsequently, the
meeting recessed and reconvened at 9:34 am on Thursday, December 2, 2021. This Special
Council meeting was convened by electronic means as authorized in Part 14 of the Procedure
By-law.

WELCOME
The Mayor acknowledged we are on the unceded homelands of the Musqueam, Squamish, and
Tsleil-Waututh Nations and we thank them for having cared for this land and look forward to
working with them in partnership as we continue to build this great city together.
The Mayor also recognized the immense contributions of the City of Vancouver’s staff who work
hard every day to help make our city an incredible place to live, work, and play.

ACKNOWLEDGEMENT - International Day of Persons with Disabilities
The Mayor noted that December 3, 2021, is International Day of Persons with Disabilities and
recognized the contributions of the Persons with Disabilities Advisory Committee members for
providing advice and guidance to staff and Council about improving accessibility, inclusion, and
universality and removing barriers that prevent the full participation of persons with disabilities in
community life.
The Mayor also recognized the tremendous historical and current work of local people with
disabilities, advocates and non-profit organizations throughout Vancouver who work every day
to create an accessible city for all.
It was noted that on December 3, 2021, City Hall and Burrard Bridge will be illuminated in purple
to mark this occasion.

Council heard from eighty-four speakers on the 2022 Capital and Operating Budget; fifteen who
spoke in support of the recommendations with suggestions, thirty-four who spoke in opposition to
the recommendations, and thirty-five who identified their position as other and provided comments
on the 2022 budget. Concerns raised included the police budget being over-funded, the police
budget being under-funded, funding required for reconciliation, high property taxes, cut library
hours, safety, inequality, work needing to be done on the age friendly city initiative, and climate
change. Suggestions included support for vacancy control regulations in single room
accommodations, and support for becoming a Restorative City.
