SPECIAL COUNCIL MEETING MINUTES
(BUSINESS LICENCE HEARING)
JANUARY 29, 2025
A Special Meeting of the Council of the City of Vancouver was held on Wednesday, January 29,
2025, at 9:35 am, in the Council Chamber, Third Floor, City Hall, for the purpose of holding a
Business Licence Hearing to consider the decision of the Chief Licence Inspector to suspend
the 2024 business licence (“Business Licence”) issued to Ricarda Bentham (the “Licencee”) for
a short term rental accommodation located at 3703-1372 Seymour Street (the “Premises”). This
Special Council meeting was convened in person and via electronic means as authorized by
Part 14 of the Procedure By-law and the Vancouver Charter.

Iain Dixon, Litigation Counsel, was present on behalf of the City of Vancouver. Ricarda
Bentham, the Licencee, was present and accompanied by Phil Dougan, Lawyer, Citadel Law
Corporation, the Licencees’ Counsel.
Mr. Dixon provided opening remarks and reviewed relevant legislation as set out in section 275
of the Vancouver Charter and section 25.1(3) of Licence By-law No. 4450.
In support of the allegations set out in the evidence brief, Mr. Dixon called Sarah Hicks, Chief
Licence Inspector, as a witness for the City of Vancouver.
Mr. Dixon along with Panel members directed questions to the City’s witness.
The Licencee’s Counsel also directed questions to the City’s witness.
The Licencee’s Counsel provided opening remarks and responded to the allegations submitted
by the City’s witness.
The Licencee’s Counsel responded to questions from the Panel and Mr. Dixon.
The Licencee’s Counsel called the following witnesses:
• Lianne Kefalas
• Ricarda Bentham, the Licencee
Ricarda Bentham and Lianne Kefalas responded to questions from the Panel and Mr. Dixon.

* * * * *
PANEL MEMBERS DISCUSSION
MOVED by Councillor Klassen
SECONDED by Councillor Dominato
THAT the Business Licence Hearing Panel uphold the decision of the Chief Licence
Inspector to suspend the 2024 business licence (“Business Licence”) issued to Ricarda
Bentham (the “Licencee”) on the basis that the evidence indicates the Licencee has not
met the requirements of the Licence By-law;
FURTHER THAT due to new claims presented by the Licencee about having clothing
and food storage on site, the Panel recommends that staff considers this and other
evidence on the issuance of a 2025 business licence.
CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY (Vote No. 10491)

MOVED by Councillor Klassen
