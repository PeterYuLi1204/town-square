SPECIAL COUNCIL MEETING MINUTES
(BUSINESS LICENCE HEARING)
APRIL 16, 2024
A Special Meeting of the Council of the City of Vancouver was held on Tuesday, April 16, 2024,
at 9:32 am, in the Council Chamber, Third Floor, City Hall, for the purpose of holding a Business
Licence Hearing to consider the decision of the Chief Licence Inspector to refuse to issue a
business licence to Anuj Chopra for the business premises located at 2807-688 Abbott Street.
This Special Council meeting was convened by electronic means as authorized by Part 14 of
the Procedure By-law and the Vancouver Charter.
PRESENT: Councillor Lisa Dominato, Chair

Iain Dixon, Lawyer and Assistant Director, Regulatory Litigation, Legal Services, was present on
behalf of the City of Vancouver. Anuj Chopra, the Applicant, was present on their own behalf.
Mr. Dixon provided opening remarks and reviewed relevant legislation as set out in section 275
of the Vancouver Charter and sections 3 (6), 4(3) of Licence By-law No. 4450.
In support of the allegations set out in the evidence brief, Mr. Dixon called Sarah Hicks Chief
Licence Inspector, as a witness.
* * * * *
The Committee recessed at 9:35 am and reconvened at 9:41 am.
* * * * *
Mr. Dixon along with Panel members directed questions to the witness.

Special Council Meeting (Business Licence Hearing)
Minutes, Tuesday, April 16, 2024 3
The Applicant did not direct questions to the witness.
The Applicant provided opening remarks and responded to the allegations submitted by the
witness.
The Applicant responded to questions from the Panel and Mr. Dixon.
Mr. Dixon provided closing submissions based on the evidence and reviewed the reasons for
the business licence denial.
The Applicant provided closing submissions.
PANEL MEMBERS DISCUSSION
MOVED by Councillor Bligh
SECONDED by Councillor Boyle
THAT the Business License Hearing Panel uphold the decision of the Chief License
Inspector to refuse to issue the Business Licence to the Applicant pursuant to s. 4(3) of
the Licence By-law on the basis that:
• The license applicant has two convictions within the past five years for operating
a short-term accommodation premises without a license.
CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY
