SPECIAL COUNCIL MEETING MINUTES
(BUSINESS LICENCE HEARING)
JULY 15, 2025
A Special Meeting of the Council of the City of Vancouver was held on Tuesday, July 15, 2025,
at 9:31 am, in the Council Chamber, Third Floor, City Hall, for the purpose of holding a Business
Licence Hearing to consider the decision of the Chief Licence Inspector to suspend the 2024
business licence (“Business Licence”) issued to Deeno Mazhari (the “Licencee”) for business
premises located at 1907-1050 Burrard Street, Vancouver, British Columbia (the “Premises”).
This Special Council meeting was convened in person and via electronic means as authorized
by Part 14 of the Procedure By-law and the Vancouver Charter.

Iain Dixon, Assistant Director - Regulatory Litigation, Legal Services, was present on behalf of
the City of Vancouver. Deeno Mazhari, the Licencee, was present and accompanied by James
Christie, Lawyer at Lesperance Mendes Lawyers, the Licencee’s Counsel.
Mr. Dixon provided opening remarks and reviewed relevant legislation as set out in section 275
of the Vancouver Charter and section 28.1 of Licence By-law No. 4450.
In support of the allegations set out in the evidence brief, Mr. Dixon called Alina Urloiu, Deputy
Chief Licence Inspector as a witness for the City of Vancouver.
Mr. Dixon along with Panel members directed questions to the City’s witness.
The Licencee’s Counsel also directed questions to the City’s witness.
The Licencee’s Counsel provided opening remarks and responded to the allegations submitted
by the City’s witness.
The Licencee’s Counsel called Deeno Mazhari, the Licencee as a witness.
The Licencee’s Counsel along with Panel members directed questions to the witness.
The witness responded to questions from Mr. Dixon.
Mr. Dixon provided closing submissions based on the evidence and reviewed the reasons for
the business licence suspension.
The Licencee’s Counsel provided closing submissions.
The panel members asked additional questions of City Counsel and the Deputy Chief Licence
Inspector.
PANEL MEMBERS DISCUSSION
MOVED by Councillor Dominato

Special Council Meeting (Business Licence Hearing)
Minutes, Tuesday, July 15, 2025 3
SECONDED by Councillor Klassen
THAT the Business Licence Hearing Panel deny the application made by Deeno
Mazhari (the “Licencee”) for a 2025 business licence (“Business Licence”) of a short-
term rental accommodation at 1907 – 1050 Burrard Street, on the basis that the
evidence presented does not establish the property at 1907-1050 Burrard Street as a
Principal Residence, as defined by the City as the usual dwelling unit where an
individual lives, makes their home and conducts their daily affairs, and is a condition of
their short term rental business licence.
CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY (Vote No. 10866)
