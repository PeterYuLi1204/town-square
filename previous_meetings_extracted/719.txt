REPORT TO COUNCIL
AUDITOR GENERAL RECRUITMENT COMMITTEE
DECEMBER 4, 2020
A meeting of the Auditor General Recruitment Committee was held on Friday,
December 4, 2020 at 1:02 pm, in the Council Chamber, Third Floor, City Hall. This
Council meeting was convened by electronic means as authorized under Part 14 of the
Procedure By-law and Vancouver Charter and the Minister of Public Safety and Solicitor
General of the Province of British Columbia – Emergency Program Act, updated
Ministerial Order No. M192.
PRESENT: Councillor Rebecca Bligh
Councillor Adriane Carr
Councillor Lisa Dominato
Councillor Colleen Hardwick
Councillor Sarah Kirby-Yung
CITY CLERK’S OFFICE: Rosemary Hagiwara, Acting City Clerk
Tina Penney, Deputy City Clerk
Rowena Choi, Meeting Coordinator
WELCOME
The Deputy City Clerk called the meeting to order.
1. Selection of Chair and Vice-Chair
MOVED by Councillor Carr
THAT the Committee recommend to Council
THAT the following be appointed Chair and Vice-Chair of the Auditor General
Recruitment Committee, for the term December 4, 2020, to March 12, 2021:
• Councillor Hardwick (Chair)
• Councillor Bligh (Vice-Chair)
carried
AMENDMENT MOVED by Councillor Carr
THAT the words “the following be appointed Chair and Vice-Chair” be struck and
replaced with the phrase, “recommend that Council make the following
appointments”.
LOST (Vote No. 06731)
(Councillors Dominato, Hardwick and Kirby-Yung opposed)

Auditor General Recruitment Committee
Minutes, Friday, December 04, 2020 2
The amendment having failed, the original motion was clarified by Legal staff and the
Acting City Clerk regarding the process of the appointment of Chair and Vice-Chair.
Following the receipt of additional information, Councillors Hardwick and Bligh accepted
the appointment of Chair and Vice-Chair, respectively. Subsequently, the motion was put
and CARRIED (Vote No. 06732) with Councillor Kirby-Yung opposed.
FINAL MOTION AS APPROVED
THAT the following be appointed Auditor General Recruitment Committee Chair
and Vice-Chair, for the term December 4, 2020, to March 12, 2021:
• Councillor Hardwick (Chair)
• Councillor Bligh (Vice-Chair)
* * * * *
At this point in the meeting, Councillor Hardwick assumed the role of Chair.
The Chair acknowledged we are on the unceded territories of the Musqueam, Squamish,
and Tsleil-Waututh Nations and we thank them for having cared for this land and look
forward to working with them in partnership as we continue to build this great city
together.
The Chair also recognized the immense contributions of the City of Vancouver’s staff
who work hard every day to help make our city an incredible place to live, work, and
play.

MOVED by Councillor Carr
THAT the Committee recommend to Council
A. THAT the Auditor General Recruitment Committee approve the Terms of
Reference/Code of Conduct for external liaison advisory members to the
Committee substantially in the form attached as Appendix “A” to the
Report dated November 25, 2020, entitled “Approval of Terms of
Reference for External Representatives on the Auditor General
Recruitment Committee”.
B. THAT the Auditor General Recruitment Committee instruct the Chief
Procurement Officer to prepare a Request for Proposals (“RFP”) prior to
end of Q1 2021 designed to seek proposals from qualified recruitment
firms to assist with the recruitment of the Auditor General.
C. THAT, the Chief Procurement Officer provide the Request for Proposals
(“RFP”) to the Auditor General Recruitment Committee and City Solicitor
for review and comment prior to the end of Q1 2021, and to, upon
approval of the RFP by the Committee, to issue and publicly post the RFP
and, following the closing date for the RFP and the receipt of proposals,
to submit all compliant proposals to the Committee for evaluation.
D. THAT, upon the Chief Procurement Officer being advised of the
successful proponent by the Auditor General Recruitment Committee, to
award the Recruiting Services Contract (the “contract”) for Auditor
General recruiting services to such proponent and notify the Committee
when such contract has been awarded and executed.
E. THAT, the Chair of the Auditor General Recruitment Committee and City
Solicitor be authorized to execute and deliver the Recruiting Services
Contract on behalf of the City.
F. THAT, no legal rights or obligations will be created or arise as a result of
the Auditor General Recruitment Committee’s approval of the Request for

B. THAT the Auditor General Recruitment Committee direct the Chief
Procurement Officer to provide the Committee with the pre-qualified
