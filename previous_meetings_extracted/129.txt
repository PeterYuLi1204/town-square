WELCOME
The Chair acknowledged we are on the unceded homelands of the Musqueam, Squamish, and
Tsleil-Waututh People. We thank them for having cared for this land and look forward to working
with them in partnership as we continue to build this great city together.
The Chair also recognized the immense contributions of the City of Vancouver’s staff who work
hard every day to help make our city an incredible place to live, work, and play.

Auditor General Committee
Minutes, Thursday, February 13, 2025 2
1. Additional Information on the December 2024 Follow-Up Report
The Auditor General introduced the item and the Chair of Finance and Risk Committee,
Vancouver Police Board, responded to questions.
2. 2024 Office of the Auditor General Annual Report and 2025 Operational Plan
The Auditor General introduced the item and provided a presentation, and along with the
Deputy Auditor General responded to questions.
MOVED by Councillor Klassen
SECONDED by Councillor Montague
THAT the Committee recommend to Council
THAT the Auditor General Committee receive the Auditor General’s Report dated
January 31, 2025, entitled “2024 Office of the Auditor General Annual Report and
2025 Operational Plan”.
CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY (Vote No. 10524)
At 10:25 am, during debate on Item 2, Chair Dominato relinquished the Chair to Vice-Chair
Kirby-Yung in order to participate in debate and resumed as Chair once finished.
3. 2024 Annual Whistleblower Report
The Auditor General introduced the item, and Audit Principal Flanagan provided a presentation.
The aforementioned, along with the City Manager and the Chief Human Resources Officer,
responded to questions.
MOVED by Councillor Kirby-Yung
SECONDED by Councillor Klassen
THAT the Committee recommend to Council
THAT the Auditor General Committee receive the Auditor General’s Report dated
February 6, 2025, entitled “2024 Whistleblower Report;”
FURTHER THAT the Auditor General Committee endorse the above-noted
report’s 17 recommendations.
CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY (Vote No. 10525)
