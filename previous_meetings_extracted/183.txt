SPECIAL COUNCIL MEETING MINUTES
(BUSINESS LICENCE HEARING)
OCTOBER 10, 2024
A Special Meeting of the Council of the City of Vancouver was held on Thursday, October
10, 2024, at 9:30 am, in the Council Chamber, Third Floor, City Hall, for the purpose of holding
a Business Licence Hearing to consider the decision of the Chief Licence Inspector to not
support the issuance of a 2024 business licence to Kevin Wong and Jacqueline Fong for a short
term rental accommodation located at 2749 E 8th Avenue. This Special Council meeting was
convened by electronic means as authorized by Part 14 of the Procedure By-law and the
Vancouver Charter.
PRESENT: Councillor Brian Montague, Chair
Councillor Adriane Carr
Councillor Mike Klassen
CITY CLERK’S OFFICE: David Yim, Meeting Coordinator
WELCOME
The Chair acknowledged we are on the unceded homelands of the Musqueam, Squamish, and
Tsleil-Waututh People. We thank them for having cared for this land and look forward to
working with them in partnership as we continue to build this great city together.

Robert LeBlanc, Litigation Counsel, Legal Services, was present on behalf of the City of
Vancouver. Kevin Wong and Jacqueline Fong, the Licencees, were present to represent
themselves.
Mr. LeBlanc provided opening remarks and reviewed relevant legislation as set out in section
277 of the Vancouver Charter and sections 4(12) of the City of Vancouver’s Licence By-law No.
4450.
In support of the allegations set out in the above-noted evidence, Mr. LeBlanc called Sarah
Hicks, Chief Licence Inspector, as a witness for the City of Vancouver.
Mr. LeBlanc, along with the panel member, directed questions to the City of Vancouver’s
witness. Mr. Wong also posed questions to the witness.

Special Council Meeting (Business Licence Hearing)
Minutes, Thursday, October 10, 2024 3
Mr. Wong provided opening remarks and responded to the allegations submitted by the witness.
Mr. LeBlanc provided closing submissions based on the evidence and reviewed the reasons for
the business licence suspension.
Mr. Wong provided closing submissions.

The Committee recessed at 10:46 am and reconvened at 10:51 am.

PANEL MEMBERS DISCUSSION
MOVED by Councillor Klassen
SECONDED by Councillor Carr
THAT the Business Licence Hearing Panel uphold the decision of the Chief License
Inspector to not support the issuance of the Business Licence to the Licencees for a
short-term rental accommodation located at 2749 E 8th Avenue, on the basis of the
totality of the evidence provided to the panel for this hearing.
CARRIED (Vote No.10254)
ADJOURNMENT
MOVED by Councillor Carr
SECONDED by Councillor Klassen
THAT the meeting be adjourned.
CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY
