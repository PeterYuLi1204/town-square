1. Addressing Anti-Black Racism and Historic Discrimination: Interim Report
September 15, 2022
Staff from Arts, Culture and Community Services provided a presentation and responded to
questions.
The Committee heard from twelve speakers who spoke in support of the recommendations,
shared their lived experience, and provided guidance for future work.

Special Council Meeting
Minutes, Tuesday, September 27, 2022 2
MOVED by Councillor Fry
SECONDED by Councillor Kirby-Yung
A. THAT Council receive the interim Report dated September 15, 2022, entitled
“Addressing Anti-Black Racism and Historic Discrimination: Interim Report” as an
update of work to date on addressing anti-Black racism and historic
discrimination.
B. THAT Council direct staff to conduct further community conversations with a
broader cross section of Vancouver’s Black and African diaspora communities to
inform the finalization of the City’s anti-Black racism and cultural redress strategy,
to be presented to Council in late 2023. Funding needed to continue this work will
be addressed in the development of the 2023 operating budget.
C. THAT Council direct staff to create partnerships with academics and historians to
carry out research and analysis of the history of Black and African diaspora
communities in Vancouver to be used to create awareness and support cultural
redress of historical wrongs and legacy of anti-Black racism in Vancouver.
D. THAT Council direct staff to allocate annual funding through the City’s Arts,
Culture and Community Services and other departmental granting programs to
community initiatives that further the City’s anti-Black racism response.
E. THAT Council request the Mayor, as Chair of the Vancouver Police Board, to
share the interim Report dated September 15, 2022, entitled “Addressing
Anti-Black Racism and Historic Discrimination: Interim Report” and provisional
priority actions contained in the same report for information with the Vancouver
Police Board.
F. THAT Council direct staff to share the interim Report dated September 15, 2022,
entitled “Addressing Anti-Black Racism and Historic Discrimination: Interim
Report” and provisional priority actions contained in the same report, for
information with other governmental and public organizations including, Canada
Mortgage and Housing Corporation, Provincial Parliamentary Secretary for Anti-
Racism Initiatives, Attorney General and Minister responsible for Housing,
Minister of Children and Family Development, Minister of Education BC Human
Rights Commissioner, Office of the Police Complaint Commissioner, Independent
Investigations Office, BC Housing, TransLink, Transit Police Board, Vancouver
Coastal Health, Vancouver School Board, Vancouver Public Library, Vancouver
Park Board, the Union of British Columbia Municipalities (UBCM), and the Metro
Vancouver Social Issues Committee.
CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY (Vote No. 08782)

3. Sunset Seniors’ Centre: Federal Contribution and Approval of Capital Budget
September 15, 2022
MOVED by Councillor Kirby-Yung
SECONDED by Councillor Carr
THAT Council approve the creation of a multi-year capital budget for the Sunset Seniors’
Centre project of $14,573,600, which is to be funded through:
a. $10,573,600 in Partnership contribution from the Federal government (source:
Investing in Canada Infrastructure Program); and
b. $4,000,000 in Development contribution from the City (source: cash Community
Amenity Contributions from the Cambie Corridor).
The 2019-2022 Capital Plan amount for the Sunset Seniors’ Centre project will be
increased from $3,500,000 to $14,573,600 as a result of the aforementioned Federal
Government funding and a $500,000 increase of the City funding allocation.
Expenditures for 2022 will be managed within the current overall annual capital
expenditure budget.
