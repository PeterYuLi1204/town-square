SPECIAL COUNCIL MEETING MINUTES
(BUSINESS LICENCE HEARING)
NOVEMBER 23, 2022
A Special Meeting of the Council of the City of Vancouver was held on Wednesday,
November 23, 2022, at 6:02 pm, in the Council Chamber, Third Floor, City Hall, for the purpose
of holding a Business Licence Hearing to consider the decision of the Chief Licence Inspector to
suspend the business licence (“Business Licence”) issued to Puneet Kochar (the “Licencee”),
for a short-term rental accommodation at 802 - 183 Keefer Place (the “Premises”). This Special
Council meeting was convened by electronic means as authorized by Part 14 of the Procedure
By-law and the Vancouver Charter.

Special Council Meeting (Business Licence Hearing)
Minutes, Wednesday, November 23, 2022 3
Robert LeBlanc, Lawyer, Legal Services, was present on behalf of the City of Vancouver.
Puneet Kochar, the Licencee, was present on her own behalf.
Mr. LeBlanc provided opening remarks and reviewed relevant legislation as set out in section
275 of the Vancouver Charter and section 25.1(3) of Licence By-law No. 4450.
In support of the allegations set out in the evidence brief, Mr. LeBlanc called Koji Miyaji,
Assistant Director, Community Standards, Development, Buildings and Licencing, as a witness.
Mr. LeBlanc, along with Panel members, directed questions to Mr. Miyaji.
The Licencee directed questions to Mr. Miyaji.
The Licencee provided opening remarks in defense of the allegations and responded to
questions from Panel members.
Mr. LeBlanc provided closing submissions
The Licencee provided closing submissions.
PANEL MEMBERS DISCUSSION
MOVED by Councillor Fry
SECONDED by Councillor Zhou
THAT the Business Licence Hearing Panel suspend the 2022 Business Licence issued
to Puneet Kochar (the “Licencee”) for a short-term rental accommodation at
802 - 183 Keefer Place (the “Premises”), on the basis that:
• the unit is not the primary residence of the Licencee as evidenced by the
investigation of the Premises and the listings; and
• additionally, the Licencee’s advertisement of an unlicensed long-term rental
accommodation in response to their short-term license suspension.
CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY (Vote No. 08888)
ADJOURNMENT
MOVED by Councillor Zhou
SECONDED by Councillor Fry

CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY
