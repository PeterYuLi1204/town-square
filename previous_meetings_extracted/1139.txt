REPORT REFERENCE
1. Regulating Short-Term Rentals in Vancouver
July 5, 2017
Kaye Krishna, General Manager of Development, Buildings and Licensing, and Kathryn Holm,
Director of Licensing, Property Use and Animal Services, presented the Policy Report dated
July 5, 2017, entitled “Regulating Short-Term Rentals in Vancouver”, and responded to
questions.
MOVED by Councillor Stevenson
SECONDED by Councillor Jang
A. THAT the Director of Planning be instructed to apply to amend the Zoning and
Development By-law and the Zoning and Development Fees By-law to
implement the recommendations set out in the Policy Report dated July 5,
2017, entitled “Regulating Short-Term Rentals in Vancouver” and that the
application be referred to Public Hearing.
B. THAT the Director of Legal Services be instructed to prepare amendments to
the Zoning and Development By-law, Licence By-law, Ticket Offences By-law,
and Zoning and Development Fees By-law to implement the recommendations
set out in the Policy Report dated July 5, 2017, entitled “Regulating Short-Term
Rentals in Vancouver”, for consideration at public hearing.
C. THAT the Director of Legal Services be instructed to bring forward the
amendments to the Licence By-law and the Ticket Offences By-law to
implement the recommendations set out in the Policy Report dated July 5,
2017, entitled “Regulating Short-Term Rentals in Vancouver”, at the time of
enactment of the amendments to the Zoning and Development By-law and the
Zoning and Development Fees By-law.
CARRIED (Vote No. 02090)
(Councillors Affleck, Ball, and De Genova opposed)

2. 2017 Homelessness Action Week (HAW) Grants
May 25, 2017
Jennifer Hales, Community Services, presented the Administrative Report dated May 25,
2017, entitled “2017 Homelessness Action Week (HAW) Grants” and responded to questions.
MOVED by Councillor Jang
SECONDED by Councillor Carr
A. THAT Council champion Vancouver’s 2017 Homelessness Action Week (HAW) by
supporting neighbourhood responses to homelessness.
B. THAT Council approve an allocation of $43,000 in grants as outlined in
Appendix A of the Administrative Report dated May 25, 2017, entitled “2017
Homelessness Action Week (HAW) Grants” to create awareness about and
engage citizens on solutions to homelessness through a variety of events during
Homelessness Action Week (October 8 – 14, 2017) and throughout the year;
source of funds to be the 2017 Community Services Operating Budget – Street
Homelessness.
C. THAT Council direct staff to report back by October 3, 2017 to proclaim
Homelessness Action Week (HAW) 2017 and showcase actions and events being
planned.
CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY AND

4. Approval of new Sub-sublease Agreement to Non-Profit Operator for Childcare
facility at 1399 Fountain Way (known as the “False Creek YMCA Childcare”)
June 27, 2017
A. THAT Council authorize the Director of Real Estate Services to negotiate and to
execute a sub-sublease agreement with The YMCA of Greater Vancouver
(“YMCA”) as the tenant and operator of the childcare facility (the “Premises”)
situated on those lands legally described as Parcel identifier: 007 281 285; Lot
50, False Creek, Plan 17420 (the “Lands”) and having a civic address of 102-
1399 Fountain Way on the following basic terms, and on terms and conditions
consistent with the Term Sheet attached as Appendix A to the Administrative
Report dated June 27, 2017, entitled “Approval of new Sub-sublease Agreement
to Non-Profit Operator for Childcare Facility at 1399 Fountain Way (Known as
the “False Creek YMCA Childcare”)”, and otherwise satisfactory to the Director
of Real Estate Services, the Director of Legal Services, and the Managing
Director of Social Policy and Projects:
i) Term and Renewal Options:
a. Term: five (5) years commencing September 1, 2017
b. Options to Renew: Two options of five (5) years each, plus a further
option of six (6) years and one month expiring September 29, 2038
ii) Rent:
Nominal base rent of Ten Dollars ($10.00) for the term and any renewal term,
payable in advance, plus applicable taxes.
Additional rent for common area maintenance of $375.00 per month plus
applicable sales taxes, payable monthly in advance on the first day of each and
every month of the Term or any renewal term, subject to increases from time
to time which are commensurate with increases under the City’s sublease (the
“Sublease”) of the Premises from The Wellington Society (the “Society”).
The Rent for the Premises represents an annual operating grant in the amount
of $4,200 per year.
B. THAT no legal rights or obligations be created or arise by Council’s adoption of
these Recommendations unless and until the sub-sublease is signed by the City

ENQUIRIES AND OTHER MATTERS
1. Staff Support for BC Wildfires
Mayor Robertson thanked staff who are helping with wildfires around the province and
encouraged residents to contribute any way they can. The City Manager also provided an
update, noting staff from Emergency Social Services and the Heavy Urban Search and Rescue
Team (HUSAR) have been dispatched to help out. He also noted that he is working with City
Managers across the Lower Mainland to ascertain what equipment and resources each
municipality has available to assist.
2. Condolences: Toronto City Councillor Pam McConnell
Councillor Louie expressed condolences to the family, friends, and colleagues of Toronto City
Councillor and Deputy Mayor Pam McConnell, noting her long public service and her advocacy
for housing and anti-poverty measures.
3. Food Delivery Regulation
Councillor Reimer noted there are increasing numbers of companies who provide food delivery
by bicycle. She noted these self-employed delivery cyclists are often young workers, and
expressed concerns regarding increasing load and traffic on bikeways, and increasing numbers
of injuries to young workers. She requested staff provide information on what is known about
this industry, what regulations under the City’s control can and do exist, and what options the
City has to ensure worker safety by advocating to the Province.

4. Reconfiguration of Creekside Park
Councillor Carr requested staff provide information on the reconfigured Creekside Park in
North False Creek specifically if and how it varies in size from the original, the loss of
