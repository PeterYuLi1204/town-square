SPECIAL COUNCIL MEETING MINUTES
(BUSINESS LICENCE HEARING)
MARCH 8, 2022
A Special Meeting of the Council of the City of Vancouver was held on Tuesday, March 8, 2022,
at 9:31 am, in the Council Chamber, Third Floor, City Hall, for the purpose of holding a Business
Licence Hearing to consider the decision of the Chief Licence Inspector to suspend the
Business Licence issued to Daniel Piecuch and Sheena Graham (the “Licencees”), for a short-
term rental accommodation at 1351 East 19th Avenue, Vancouver, British Columbia (the
“Premises”). This Council meeting was convened by electronic means as authorized under the
Part 14 of the Procedure By-law.
PRESENT: Councillor Christine Boyle, Chair
Councillor Melissa De Genova
Councillor Michael Wiebe
CITY CLERK’S OFFICE: Kirsty Colquhoun, Meeting Coordinator
Tina Hildebrandt, Meeting Coordinator

Iain Dixon, Assistant Director – Regulatory Litigation, Legal Services, was present on behalf of
the City of Vancouver. Daniel Piecuch and Sheena Graham, the Licencees, were present on
their own behalf.
Mr. Dixon provided opening remarks, reviewed relevant legislation as set out in sections 275
and 277 of the Vancouver Charter, and section 25.1(4) of Licence By-law No. 4450, to consider
the suspension of the Short Term Rental Business license of Licensees Daniel Piecuch and
Sheena Graham.
In support of the allegations set out in the evidence brief, Mr. Dixon called Koji Miyaji, Assistant
Director, Community Standards, Development, Buildings and Licencing, as a witness.
Mr. Dixon, along with the Panel, directed questions to Mr. Miyaji who, for clarity, provided
reasons why the Licencees would no longer qualify for a business licence due to having an
illegal suite and lack of permitting.
The Licencees also directed questions to Mr. Miyaji.
Daniel Piecuch and Sheena Graham, the Licencees, provided opening remarks and reviewed
the reasons for starting a short term rental. The Licencees provided a detailed summary of the
steps they took to follow procedure and communicate with the City.
The Licencees responded to questions from the Panel.
Mr. Dixon provided closing submissions based on the evidence and reviewed the reasons for
the business licence suspension.
The Licencees provided closing comments.
PANEL MEMBERS DISCUSSION
MOVED by Councillor De Genova
SECONDED by Councillor Wiebe
THAT the Business Licence Hearing Panel uphold the decision of the Chief License
Inspector to suspend the 2021 business licence (“Business Licence”) issued to Daniel
Piecuch and Sheena Graham (the “Licencees”) for a short-term rental accommodation
