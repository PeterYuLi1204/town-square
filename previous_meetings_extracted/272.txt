SPECIAL COUNCIL MEETING MINUTES
(BUSINESS LICENCE HEARING)
JANUARY 31, 2024
A Special Meeting of the Council of the City of Vancouver was held on Wednesday, January 31,
2024, at 9:30 am, in the Council Chamber, Third Floor, City Hall, for the purpose of holding a
Business Licence Hearing to consider the decision of the Chief Licence Inspector to suspend
the 2023 business licence (“Business Licence”) issued to Julee Kaye (the “Licencee”) and to
refer the Licencee to Council to decide if it should be upheld, overturned, or have conditions
attached to the Business Licence. This Special Council meeting was convened by electronic
means as authorized by Part 14 of the Procedure By-law and the Vancouver Charter.
PRESENT: Councillor Christine Boyle, Chair
Councillor Mike Klassen
Councillor Peter Meiszner

Robert LeBlanc, Litigation Counsel, Legal Services, was present on behalf of the City of
Vancouver. Julee Kaye, the Licencee, was present on her own behalf.
Mr. LeBlanc provided opening remarks and reviewed relevant legislation as set out in section
275 of the Vancouver Charter and sections 4(3), 25.1(1) and 30(1) of the City of Vancouver’s
Licence By-law No. 4450.
In support of the allegations set out in the above-noted evidence, Mr. LeBlanc called Sarah
Hicks, Chief Licence Inspector, as a witness.
Mr. LeBlanc, along with Panel members, directed questions to the witness.
The Licencee provided opening remarks and responded to the allegations submitted by the
witness.
The Licencee responded to questions from the Panel.
Mr. LeBlanc provided closing submissions based on the evidence and reviewed the reasons for
the business licence cancellation.

Special Council Meeting (Business Licence Hearing)
Minutes, Wednesday, January 31, 2024 3
The Licencee provided closing submissions.
PANEL MEMBERS DISCUSSION
MOVED by Councillor Meiszner
SECONDED by Councillor Klassen
THAT the Business Licence Hearing Panel suspend the 2023 Business Licence issued
to Julee Kaye (the “Licencee”), Business Licence 23-157001 (Short Term Rental ), for
business premises located at 4107 W 13th Avenue, Vancouver, on the basis that short-
term rental premises is not the principal residence unit of the Licencee, contrary to
section 25.1(3) of the City of Vancouver’s Licence By-law No. 4450.
CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY (Vote No. 04107)
