SPECIAL COUNCIL MEETING MINUTES
(BUSINESS LICENCE HEARING)
MARCH 1, 2023
A Special Meeting of the Council of the City of Vancouver was held on Wednesday,
March 1, 2023, at 6:03 pm, in the Council Chamber, Third Floor, City Hall, for the purpose of
holding a Business Licence Hearing to consider the decision of the Chief Licence Inspector to
cancel the business licence (“Business Licence”) issued to Chi Wah (Shirley) Pang (the
“Licencee”) for business premises located at 1397 West 41st Avenue (the “Premises”). This
Special Council meeting was convened by electronic means as authorized by Part 14 of the
Procedure By-law and the Vancouver Charter.
PRESENT: Councillor Mike Klassen, Chair

The Business Licence Hearing Panel also had before it for consideration the following additional
evidence:
• 1397 W 41 Ave visitation notes from KM (on file in the City Clerk’s Office).
Robert LeBlanc, Litigation Counsel, Legal Services, was present on behalf of the City of
Vancouver. Chi Wah (Shirley) Pang, the Licencee, was present on her own behalf.
Mr. LeBlanc provided opening remarks and reviewed relevant legislation as set out in section
275 of the Vancouver Charter and sections 4(3), 25.1(1) and 30(1) of the City of Vancouver’s
Licence By-law No. 4450.
In support of the allegations set out in the above-noted evidence, Mr. LeBlanc called Koji Miyaji,
Assistant Director, Community Standards, Development, Buildings and Licencing, as a witness.
Mr. LeBlanc, along with Panel members, directed questions to the witness.
The Licencee provided opening remarks and responded to the allegations submitted by the
witness.

Special Council Meeting (Business Licence Hearing)
Minutes, Wednesday, March 1, 2023 3
The Licencee responded to questions from the Panel.
Mr. LeBlanc provided closing submissions based on the evidence and reviewed the reasons for
the business licence cancellation.
The Licencee provided closing submissions.
PANEL MEMBERS DISCUSSION
MOVED by Councillor Dominato
SECONDED by Councillor Fry
THAT the Business Licence Hearing Panel cancel the 2023 Business Licence issued to
Chi Wah (Shirley) Pang (the “Licencee”) for a short-term rental accommodation at
1397 West 41st Avenue (the “Premises”), on the basis that:
• section 25.1(1) of the City of Vancouver’s Licence By-law No. 4450 was
breached repeatedly; Short Term Rentals took place without a Business Licence;
• the Licencee pleaded guilty in Provincial Court for breaching By-law 4450; and
• the Licencee appears to be operating a rooming house without a Business
Licence and the home has multiple unresolved life/safety issues.
CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY (Vote No. 09066)

MOVED by Councillor Fry
